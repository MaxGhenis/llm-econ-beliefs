# Referee Report: `paper.qmd`

**Recommendation:** Major revision

**Summary:** The revision is materially stronger than the earlier draft because it now states the estimand more carefully, separates canonical elasticities from simulation-facing coefficients, writes down an explicit pooling rule, and adds stability/failure appendices, but the methods case is still not fully convincing because the paper continues to rely on a prompt-conditioned response-distribution estimand without enough robustness evidence on pooling, provider comparability, or prompt sensitivity.

## Main concerns

1. **The estimand is now clearer, but the manuscript still sits between “beliefs” and prompt-conditioned responses.** The revised text does a better job than before of saying that the protocol identifies prompt-conditioned response distributions rather than stable latent priors ([`paper.qmd` lines 19-29](file:///Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/paper.qmd#L19-L29), [`paper.qmd` lines 232-240](file:///Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/paper.qmd#L232-L240)), which is a real improvement. But the title, abstract, and some prose still lean toward “what models believe,” and the paper never shows that the responses are stable enough across prompt variants to justify stronger latent-belief language. For an ML/LLM methods audience, the identification claim needs to remain narrowly protocol-based unless the paper can add stronger invariance evidence.

2. **The pooling rule is explicit, but the distributional approximation is still under-validated.** The manuscript now gives a concrete piecewise-uniform quantile-to-distribution map and an equal-weight mixture over runs ([`paper.qmd` lines 85-106](file:///Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/paper.qmd#L85-L106)), which is a substantial improvement. However, the sensitivity evidence is still modest: the stability appendix is prefix-based and the pooling appendix compares only three estimators at the rank level ([`stability-appendix.md`](file:///Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/tables/stability-appendix.md), [`pooling-robustness-appendix.md`](file:///Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/tables/pooling-robustness-appendix.md)). The paper still does not show that the substantive conclusions are stable to alternative tail assumptions, alternative interpolation rules, or a leave-one-run/leave-one-seed sensitivity analysis.

3. **Provider comparability is better documented, but failure handling still leaves an inferential gap.** The protocol section now spells out model-specific decoding choices and states that failed runs are excluded from pooled summaries ([`paper.qmd` lines 126-135](file:///Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/paper.qmd#L126-L135)), which is helpful. But the failure appendix shows that the main nontrivial failure concentration is all in `grok-4.20`, with the same structured-output error recurring across several quantities ([`grok-failures-appendix.md`](file:///Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/tables/grok-failures-appendix.md)). The manuscript does not yet explain whether parseability differences, timeout behavior, or structured-output failures could bias the cross-provider rankings, especially because the failure rate is not random across models.

4. **Prompt sensitivity is acknowledged, but the evidence is still too thin for a methods paper.** The sign-clarification example for income elasticity and the Armington clarification follow-up are both useful and were not present in the earlier draft ([`paper.qmd` lines 216-225](file:///Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/paper.qmd#L216-L225), [`armington-clarify-delta.md`](file:///Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/tables/armington-clarify-delta.md)). Still, these are targeted probes on two quantities, not a broad assessment of how prompt phrasing affects the whole elicitation pipeline. Because prompt sensitivity is part of the estimand in this paper, it needs broader evidence than two illustrative reruns.

5. **The stability evidence is directionally reassuring but not yet sufficient to justify the repeated-elicitation design choice.** Appendix Table A1 shows that 10-run prefix summaries are close to 15-run summaries on the canonical subpanel ([`stability-appendix.md`](file:///Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/tables/stability-appendix.md)), which is good. But prefix stability is not the same as convergence of the elicited distribution, and the manuscript does not provide a rationale for why 15 runs per cell is enough beyond a pragmatic statement. For a methods contribution, I would expect a more explicit argument for run count selection or a more formal stability criterion.

6. **The mixed panel is better separated than before, but the headline claims still rely on a narrow subset of the full design.** The manuscript now clearly separates canonical elasticities from PolicyEngine-style response coefficients and reports those in different tables ([`paper.qmd` lines 67-83](file:///Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/paper.qmd#L67-L83), [`paper.qmd` lines 166-224](file:///Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/paper.qmd#L166-L224)). That is an improvement. Even so, the paper’s main empirical story depends on the canonical subpanel, while the full dataset still contains implementation-facing parameters and a labeled legacy row; the manuscript should be explicit that the headline “model ordering” is a subpanel result, not a universal ordering across all elicited quantities.

## Strengths

1. The revised manuscript is materially clearer about the estimand than the prior version. It now says the protocol identifies prompt-conditioned response distributions rather than claiming to recover a stable latent prior, which is the right conceptual move for this literature.

2. The pooling section is a real improvement. Writing down the quantile-to-distribution construction and the equal-weight mixture makes the uncertainty object reproducible instead of hand-wavy.

3. The paper now contains genuinely useful robustness and diagnostics appendices: prefix stability, alternative pooling, tool-use, failure analysis, and the Armington clarification probe. That makes the work feel like an actual methods paper rather than just a results memo.

4. Separating canonical elasticities from simulation-facing coefficients makes the manuscript much more defensible. The earlier version mixed incompatible objects more aggressively; the current split is the right direction.

## Highest-value revision

Add one compact robustness appendix that combines three checks on the canonical subpanel: a leave-one-provider-out analysis, a stronger prompt-perturbation sweep on 2-3 ambiguous quantities, and a sensitivity comparison of the pooled distributions under at least one alternative quantile-to-distribution rule. If the main ordering and uncertainty rankings survive those checks, the paper becomes much more convincing as an ML/LLM methods contribution.
