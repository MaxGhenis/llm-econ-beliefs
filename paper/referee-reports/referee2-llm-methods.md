**Overall recommendation**

Weak reject.

The question is interesting and the data collection effort is real, but the current paper does not yet identify “model belief” cleanly enough for the methodological claims it wants to make. In its current form, the evidence supports the weaker claim that different models give different numeric answers under one elicitation protocol, not the stronger claim that the paper has measured stable, policy-relevant latent priors.

**Major comments**

1. The core identification assumption behind repeated sampling and uncertainty pooling is not defended. The paper takes 15 runs, reconstructs a within-run distribution, and then forms an equal-weight mixture as the model-level object, but repeated black-box generations are not obviously i.i.d. draws from a latent belief distribution; they also reflect decoder randomness, prompt under-specification, provider defaults, and parser/success filtering. I do not see temperature, `top_p`, seed control, fresh-session vs same-session protocol, or any argument that `R=15` is enough for the mixture to stabilize. As written, the pooled interval looks as much like a property of the generation pipeline as a property of the model. See [paper.qmd:85](/Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/paper.qmd#L85), [paper.qmd:94](/Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/paper.qmd#L94), [paper.qmd:102](/Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/paper.qmd#L102), [paper.qmd:120](/Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/paper.qmd#L120).

2. Prompt design is under-specified, and the one reported robustness check actually demonstrates fragility. The paper never shows the actual prompt text or the quantity-specific interpretation instructions; it only lists requested JSON fields. Asking for `citations` and `reasoning_summary` in the same response as quantiles may itself perturb the numeric elicitation and likely interacts with provider-specific reasoning behavior. More importantly, a small sign clarification flips `grok-4.1-fast` from `+0.09` to `-0.097`, which is not a minor footnote but evidence that the measured object is highly prompt-dependent. Several sign-fixed pooled intervals still include positive values, so the ambiguity is not fully resolved. See [paper.qmd:49](/Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/paper.qmd#L49), [paper.qmd:58](/Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/paper.qmd#L58), [paper.qmd:61](/Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/paper.qmd#L61), [paper.qmd:225](/Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/paper.qmd#L225), [income-signfix-delta.md:10](/Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/tables/income-signfix-delta.md#L10), [income-signfix-delta.md:14](/Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/tables/income-signfix-delta.md#L14).

3. Cross-model comparability is asserted rather than established. “Same quantities, same prompt family, same number of repeated runs” is not enough when the compared systems differ in hidden system prompts, reasoning modes, default sampling settings, API behavior, and model version stability; several are explicitly preview models. In addition, `grok-4.20` has a materially lower success rate, but the paper does not analyze whether failures are concentrated on hard quantities or unstable responses. Conditioning on successful structured outputs can bias both centers and interval widths, so some of the cross-provider differences may be selection effects. See [paper.qmd:106](/Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/paper.qmd#L106), [paper.qmd:114](/Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/paper.qmd#L114), [paper.qmd:120](/Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/paper.qmd#L120), [paper.qmd:133](/Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/paper.qmd#L133), [model-overview.md:6](/Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/tables/model-overview.md#L6).

4. The reported results do not yet warrant the stronger claims about systematic, policy-relevant between-model differences. Table 2 shows that for several headline quantities, cross-model spread in pooled centers is smaller than the mean pooled 90% width: Armington `1.0` vs `5.014`, ETI `0.147` vs `1.024`, wage elasticity `0.1` vs `0.651`. That makes the between-model separation look weak relative to the within-model uncertainty object the paper itself emphasizes. Yet the paper reports average ranks with no uncertainty on those ranks and then interprets them as economically meaningful differences. I am also unconvinced by the “responsiveness-heavy” summary, because it ranks models by the magnitude of pooled point estimates across heterogeneous and sign-sensitive quantities; a wrong-sign answer can look “more responsive” than a plausible small positive one. See [paper.qmd:159](/Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/paper.qmd#L159), [paper.qmd:177](/Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/paper.qmd#L177), [paper.qmd:201](/Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/paper.qmd#L201), [quantity-disagreement.md:6](/Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/tables/quantity-disagreement.md#L6), [quantity-disagreement.md:12](/Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/tables/quantity-disagreement.md#L12), [quantity-disagreement.md:13](/Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/tables/quantity-disagreement.md#L13), [model-overview.md:3](/Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/tables/model-overview.md#L3).

5. The calibration and validity discussion is too weak for a methods paper. The manuscript correctly says pooled interval width is not calibration, but then still interprets narrow intervals as models behaving as if the literature is “tight and settled” and wide intervals as substantive openness. Without either external calibration on resolved numeric tasks or strong internal validity checks, interval width is just a feature of this elicitation setup. The draft itself explicitly says the hierarchical sensitivity subsection and calibration section are still to be added, which makes the current submission feel premature. See [paper.qmd:197](/Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/paper.qmd#L197), [paper.qmd:243](/Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/paper.qmd#L243), [paper.qmd:259](/Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/paper.qmd#L259), [draft.md:264](/Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/draft.md#L264), [draft.md:266](/Users/maxghenis/llm-econ-beliefs/paper/draft.md#L266).

**Concrete revision requests**

- Add a full prompt appendix with the exact system/user prompts, quantity-specific wording, output schema, API parameters, session-reset protocol, and parse/repair/exclusion rules.
- Run a prompt-sensitivity study on a nontrivial subset of quantities: remove `citations`/`reasoning_summary`, vary sign wording, vary interpretation wording, and show how rank orderings and pooled intervals move.
- Replace or supplement the equal-weight mixture summary with inference on model differences: bootstrap over runs, hierarchical modeling, or pairwise probabilities that one model’s center exceeds another’s for each quantity.
- Redesign the headline comparison metric so it is sign-aware and scale-aware; do not summarize “responsiveness” by absolute pooled centers across heterogeneous quantities.
- Add calibration and validity checks: resolved numeric benchmark tasks, internal consistency checks for quantiles, and evidence that repeated-run variability is stable and not dominated by decoder settings.
- Analyze missing/failed runs by model and quantity, especially for `grok-4.20`, and show that the conclusions are not driven by conditioning on successful outputs only.
